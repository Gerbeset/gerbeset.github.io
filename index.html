Biography:

I was born in Wyoming and was raised across the border in Sturgis South Dakota. In high school, I was active in music, theather, speech and debate, and Boy Scouts. After high school, I took a gap year and worked in the Senate as an intern in Washington D.C. I worked for Senators John Thune (SD) and Steve Daines (MT). I then attended the University of South Dakota (Go Yotes!) where I double majored in mathematics and philosophy. Now I am a Ph.D. student in mathematics at Texas A&M University under the supervision of Dr. Jean-Luc Guermond. My life's passion is solving problems with logic and reason, and I think mathematics is one of best ways of doing so.
